The Art of Urer

The police will catch up with me eventually. They do with a lot of murderers. We’re imperfect; damaged. Evidence inevitably stacks up against us. If the crime don’t getcha, the cover-up will.

Take your common murderers, full of hate, greed, envy. Kill the victim, perhaps grab the money, leave evidence. Whoops. They’re of pretty average intelligence, obviously; they’d find better ways to cope, and get what they’re after, otherwise.

Then you’ve got your serial killers who try to be smarter. Every week they go uncaptured is another week to feed into their delusions of invincibility. Their intelligence and discipline goes to pot, and they get sloppy. They’re addicts, really; they’re addicted to the whole ritual, the self-delusion, the fantasy.

I like to think I have flair. As though violently ending the life of an innocent was an art I was elevating! But if I said that I’d be one of those sad acts who saw a film with the Joker in it and thought ‘me too!’ No. I just like killing, and the arty angle is just to amuse myself and to mess with other people. There’s no inspiration, no grand vision. No illusion. Just kicks.

I’m sure they will be very clever, or least put in good, thorough police work. Clothing fibres, DNA, it gets onto the scene, try as you might. You can’t remove it all. So I just try to make it difficult for the detectives not to contaminate the evidence. It’s not a perfect tactic. A good team will get what they’re after, if they stick to procedure.

If.

But people have their urges, the sort that they have to fight just that little bit harder to overcome. Last time, I left the cops a burger. A really nice one. You could smell it was a good one; everything just right, cheese melting just over the edge, salad still crisp – fuck murder, burgers are an art form! I knew they’d find the victim within an hour, so I made sure the burger would still be hot. You could almost hear it sizzle. I’m sure they tried not to drool. And I just know at least one guy would have had to fight the urge to grab it and take a bite when no-one was watching. I’m such a bastard.

Now, you. I’ve got you figured out. You’re gonna love this. Not the murder bit, obviously, that’ll suck; but post-mortem? Bubble wrap. Think about it. Detective-Sergeant McWossname’ll find you, or most of you. Move closer to investigate. And he’ll see your bits, all wrapped up.

Pop. Pop. Pop. Pop.

He wouldn’t. Of course he wouldn’t. But he’d wanna.

They’d better mention that when they throw the book at me. I don’t just do this for the good of my health, you know!
