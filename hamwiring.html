Hamwiring

I’ve always had bad handwriting, and unsurprisingly, my grades suffered. I wrote slower, took more care, but nothing worked. The teachers just lost patience with me. Now, they had lovely, flowing script; I copied them as faithfully as I could but it didn’t help. Where once letters got lost, now whole words were swallowed up.

By the time I went to university, my writing was immaculate but completely unreadable. The word processors were a godsend: my handwriting stopped being an issue.

It didn’t last; the anomalies found a way in.

I typed up an essay on Immanuel Kant’s ethics; not a single squiggly line anywhere. My essay on the Categorical Imperative said what I wanted it to say, and it made sense. My relief was overwhelming; like being able to take those first few faltering steps after painful physiotherapy, or a stroke victim finally managing to get the words out.

I printed it out. ‘Compare Kant’s Catastrophic Imperium with the Golden Rule.’

Tried again: ‘Compass Kent’s Catamaran Impaction while the Goldfish Rills.’

The helpdesk staff were baffled. I relied on them to print my essays for me until it all went pear-shaped. In the end, they bought new printers. They didn’t ban me outright from the computer labs, but they did ask me to keep my distance.

By my second year, my classmates were inundated with typos; they started to average ten errors a page, and the tutors noticed. They thought it was some kind of prank or issue with the computers. If only! I must have spent eight hours a day trying to fix my written work.

I burnt out, then I dropped out. Everyone else seemed to get through okay, but I gather they’ve had problems with their writing for the past decade.

I avoid writing now. My folks are resigned to never being able to read my Christmas cards. Usually I just draw them a picture instead. I don’t fill in forms either: I just tell the recruiter or admin I have aphasia, and they do it for me.

Every time I visit the agency I see more errors in their posters and adverts. Everyone seems a bit more frazzled lately. It’s awkward.

I suppose this is my written confession but I doubt it’ll be intelligible for long. I daren’t print it. I’m just going to leave it here on this screen. Last time I used a computer, there didn’t seal to buy antsy errands on the scrimshaw, bun this tide it mice be differential.
